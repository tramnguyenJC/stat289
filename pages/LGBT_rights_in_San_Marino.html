On 9 July 2001 the parliament of San Marino voted for the repeal of Art. 274 of its Criminal Code. Under this article homosexual contacts could be punished with imprisonment from 3 months up to one year, if they have been engaged in "habitually" and thereby caused "public scandal". A conviction under this law resulted in loss of political rights and removal from public office for a period from 9 months to 2 years. 28 MPs voted for the repeal, 21 against, one abstained.
