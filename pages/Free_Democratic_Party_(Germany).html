The Free Democratic Party ( Freie Demokratische Partei , FDP) is a liberal political party in Germany . It is the successor to the former liberal parties existing before World War 2, the German People's Party , the German Democratic Party , and the National Liberal Party . Since its establishment in 1948, the party has been the third largest party in Germany, having gained between 5.8% and 14.6% of the votes in federal elections. It was the junior partner in coalition governments with the Christian Democratic Union from 1949 to 1956, from 1961 to 1966, from 1982 to 1998 and again since November 28, 2009. It had also been the junior partner in the governing coalition with the Social Democratic Party from 1969 to 1982.

Template:Infobox German Political Party The Free Democratic Party ( Freie Demokratische Partei , FDP) is a centre-right liberal political party in Germany . The party's ideology combines beliefs in individual liberty, in a state or government "that is as limited as possible and as extensive as necessary" ( so viel Staat wie nötig, so wenig Staat wie möglich! ). It promotes a market economy , with traditional features of the German social welfare system. The FDP is currently the third-largest party in the Bundestag . In foreign policy the FDP supports European integration and transatlantic partnership.
